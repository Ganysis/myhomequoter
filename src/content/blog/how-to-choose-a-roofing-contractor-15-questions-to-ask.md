## The 15 Essential Questions Every Homeowner Must Ask

### 1. "What's your license number, and can I verify it online?"

**Why this matters:** 23% of roofing contractors operate without proper licensing.

**What to do:** Write down their license number and verify it on your state's licensing board website. Look for:
- Current status (active/expired)
- Any disciplinary actions
- Date of original licensing

**Red flag:** If they hesitate, claim they "don't have it with them," or say licensing isn't required in your area.

### 2. "Can you provide proof of both liability and workers' compensation insurance?"

**Why this matters:** Without proper insurance, YOU become liable for injuries or property damage.

**What to expect:** They should provide certificates showing:
- **General liability:** Minimum $500,000 (preferably $1M+)
- **Workers' compensation:** Current and covers all crew members
- **Certificates should be recent** (issued within 60 days)

**Action item:** Call the insurance company directly to verify coverage is current.

### 3. "How long have you been operating under this business name in this area?"

**Why this matters:** Storm chasers and fly-by-night operators often use similar names to established companies.

**What you want:** 
- **3+ years** under the same business name
- **Local address** (not a P.O. Box)
- **Consistent online presence** (website, Google Business Profile)

### 4. "Can you provide 5 references from projects completed in the last 12 months?"

**Why this matters:** Recent references show current quality standards and customer service.

**Questions to ask references:**
- Was the project completed on time and budget?
- How did they handle cleanup?
- Any issues during or after installation?
- Would you hire them again?

> **Pro Tip:** Drive by 2-3 reference addresses to see the work quality firsthand. Quality contractors are proud to show off their work.

### 5. "Will you provide a detailed, written estimate with material specifications?"

**Why this matters:** Vague estimates lead to change orders and cost overruns.

**Your estimate should include:**
- **Specific material brands and models**
- **Square footage calculations**
- **Labor breakdown**
- **Permit costs**
- **Cleanup and disposal**
- **Timeline with milestones**

### 6. "What warranties do you offer on materials and labor?"

**Why this matters:** Warranties reveal confidence in workmanship and protect your investment.

**Industry standards:**
- **Materials:** 15-50 years (manufacturer warranty)
- **Labor/Installation:** 5-10 years minimum
- **Workmanship:** 2-5 years on details (flashing, vents, etc.)

**Red flag:** Contractors offering only manufacturer warranties or less than 2 years on labor.

### 7. "Will you handle all permits and inspections?"

**Why this matters:** 41% of roofing code violations occur when contractors skip proper permitting.

**What they should handle:**
- Pulling all required permits
- Scheduling inspections
- Ensuring code compliance
- Providing copies of approved permits and inspections

### 8. "What's your payment schedule?"

**Why this matters:** Unreasonable payment terms signal cash flow problems or scams.

**Reasonable payment schedule:**
- **10-15%** down payment (never more than $1,000)
- **Progress payments** tied to completed milestones
- **Final 10-20%** held until completion and cleanup

**Major red flags:**
- Demanding full payment upfront
- Only accepting cash
- Door-to-door solicitation with "today only" pricing

### 9. "Who will supervise the job site daily?"

**Why this matters:** Proper supervision prevents 67% of installation errors.

**What you want:**
- **Experienced foreman** on-site daily
- **Clear communication chain** for questions/issues
- **Regular progress updates**
- **Daily cleanup protocols**

### 10. "How do you protect my property and landscaping during the project?"

**Why this matters:** Roofing generates significant debris that can damage your property.

**Standard protection measures:**
- **Tarps over landscaping** and AC units
- **Magnetic sweepers** for nail cleanup
- **Dumpster placement** to avoid lawn damage
- **Window and siding protection**

### 11. "What happens if weather delays the project?"

**Why this matters:** Weather delays affect 78% of roofing projects.

**Professional response includes:**
- **Flexible scheduling** with realistic timelines
- **Weather protection protocols** for partially completed work
- **Clear communication** about delays
- **No penalties** for weather-related delays

### 12. "Can you explain your safety protocols?"

**Why this matters:** Roofing has one of the highest injury rates in construction.

**Safety indicators:**
- **OSHA training** for all crew members
- **Fall protection equipment** (harnesses, safety lines)
- **First aid certified** supervisor on-site
- **Safety meeting** before each job starts

### 13. "Will you provide a lien waiver upon final payment?"

**Why this matters:** Protects you from claims by subcontractors or material suppliers.

**What you need:**
- **Conditional lien waiver** upon progress payments
- **Unconditional final lien waiver** upon project completion
- **Subcontractor lien waivers** if applicable

### 14. "How do you handle change orders or additional work?"

**Why this matters:** Prevents surprise charges and scope creep.

**Professional approach:**
- **Written change orders** before additional work begins
- **Clear pricing** for common add-ons
- **Photo documentation** of unexpected issues
- **Your approval required** before proceeding

### 15. "What's your process if I'm not satisfied with the completed work?"

**Why this matters:** Shows commitment to customer satisfaction and quality.

**Quality contractors offer:**
- **Walkthrough inspection** with you before final payment
- **Punch list process** for any issues
- **Clear resolution timeline**
- **No final payment** until you're completely satisfied

## Red Flags That Disqualify Contractors Immediately

⚠️ **Instant disqualifiers:**
- Door-to-door solicitation
- Demands full payment upfront
- No local address or phone number
- Can't provide insurance certificates
- Significantly lower than other bids (30%+ below)
- High-pressure tactics or "today only" pricing
- No written contract or estimate
- Poor online reviews with similar complaints

## Expert Tips for Negotiating with Roofing Contractors

### 1. Time Your Project Strategically
**Best timing:** Late fall or early spring when contractors are less busy
**Potential savings:** 10-15% lower prices during slow periods
**Avoid:** Storm season when prices spike 20-40%

### 2. Bundle Services for Better Pricing
Many contractors offer package deals for:
- Roof replacement + gutter installation
- Multiple buildings on same property
- Referrals to neighbors

### 3. Understand the True Cost Structure
**Materials:** 40-50% of total cost
**Labor:** 30-40% of total cost
**Overhead & Profit:** 15-25% of total cost
**Permits/Disposal:** 5-10% of total cost

### 4. Protect Yourself with Contract Terms
**Include these clauses:**
- Right to inspect work at each stage
- Detailed cleanup requirements
- Penalty clauses for delays (non-weather)
- Clear change order process
- Lien waiver requirements

## When to Walk Away: Deal-Breaker Warning Signs

**Immediate red flags:**
1. **Knocks on your door** after a storm claiming to have "leftover materials"
2. **Requires full payment** before starting work
3. **Can't provide local references** from the past year
4. **Quotes are 30%+ below** other estimates without clear explanation
5. **Pushy sales tactics** or pressure to "sign today"
6. **No physical business address** or only uses P.O. Box
7. **Poor communication** during the bidding process
8. **Negative pattern in reviews** mentioning similar issues

> **Remember:** A good contractor wants you to ask questions. If they seem annoyed or evasive, that tells you everything you need to know.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How many contractors should I get quotes from?
**A:** Get 3-4 detailed quotes from licensed contractors. More than 5 becomes overwhelming, fewer than 3 doesn't give you enough comparison data. Focus on quality over quantity.

### Q: Is the most expensive contractor always the best choice?
**A:** Not necessarily. The best contractor offers the best **value** – quality materials, proper installation, good warranty, and fair pricing. Sometimes that's the most expensive, sometimes it's the middle option.

### Q: How long should a roof replacement take?
**A:** Most residential roofs take **2-5 days** depending on size, complexity, and weather. Be suspicious of contractors who promise to finish a whole house in one day or quote timelines longer than a week for standard projects.

### Q: Should I be present during the roofing work?
**A:** You don't need to supervise constantly, but **daily check-ins** are wise. Good contractors welcome questions and provide progress updates. Schedule the final walkthrough before making final payment.

### Q: What if I discover problems after the contractor leaves?
**A:** Contact them immediately with photos of the issue. Quality contractors will return promptly to address legitimate concerns. This is why the warranty and their local reputation matter so much.

### Q: Can I negotiate the price after getting estimates?
**A:** Yes, but focus on **value additions** rather than just price cuts. Ask about upgraded materials, extended warranties, or additional services. Quality contractors have less room to negotiate on price without cutting corners.

### Q: How do I verify a contractor's insurance is current?
**A:** Call the insurance company directly using the phone number on the certificate (not the number the contractor gives you). Ask specifically if coverage is current and includes the work dates for your project.
